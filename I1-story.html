The Story of Vajrakumär

Section I.1 from JAB Manual 5th edition 2024

In the city of Tumbivan, a rich Brahmin named Dhangiri and his beautiful wife Sunandä lived a joyful life. When Sunandä became pregnant, she had a wonderful dream that her husband, a scholar, said meant she would have a bright and charming child.

One day, a Jain Ächärya named Singhiri arrived. His sermons deeply moved Dhangiri, who lost interest in worldly life and decided to become a monk. Despite his wife's shock and pleas to stay for their coming child, Dhangiri's mind was made up. He made financial arrangements for his family, and Sunandä, understanding religion deeply, accepted his decision.

A few months later, Sunandä gave birth to a handsome, smiling boy. But her happiness was short-lived. One day, a neighbor remarked how much more extravagantly Dhangiri would have celebrated the birth if he hadn't taken Dikshä.

The baby heard the word “Dikshä” and suddenly remembered his past life. He realized he was on a spiritual path and decided he must become a monk like his father. Knowing his mother wouldn't let him go, he devised a plan: he would cry constantly until she got tired of him. His plan worked perfectly. The constant crying exhausted his mother and the neighbors.

When the child was six months old, his father, now Muni Dhangiri, visited the city with Ächärya Singhiri. Fed up, Sunandä decided to give the child to his father. The Ächärya, who had special powers, had already told Muni Dhangiri to accept any "live thing" offered as alms that day.

When Muni Dhangiri arrived at his old home for alms, the baby started crying loudly. Sunandä, at her wit's end, begged him, "Please accept him so there will be peace in this house." Muni Dhangiri warned her, "Once you give him away, you will not be able to get him back." But she insisted.

She placed the child in the muni's cloth bag. Instantly, the boy stopped crying and started smiling. Surprised but determined, Sunandä watched them leave. At the Upäshray, the Ächärya saw the heavy bag and the handsome, smiling boy inside. Because he was so heavy, the Ächärya named him “Vajrakumär.” He was given to a prominent Jain family to be raised with religious teachings.

Vajrakumär was a brilliant child. By age three, he had memorized eleven Anga Ägams. One day, a friend told Sunandä how lovable and happy her son was. Her motherly love returned, and she deeply regretted her decision.

Sunandä went to get her son back, but the monks refused. Desperate, she appealed to the king. The king, after hearing the full story, arranged a test in his courthouse. He told Sunandä, "I will let Vajrakumär decide whom he wants to go with."

The next day, the court was full. Sunandä brought toys, sweets, and clothes to tempt her son. On the other side, Muni Dhangiri had only an Ogho (a monk's broom). The king explained the choice to Vajrakumär. The young boy looked at his mother's gifts, then at his father's broom. He walked over, picked up the Ogho, and began to dance with joy.

Everyone was amazed. Sunandä accepted his decision, and Vajrakumär's Dikshä was celebrated with joy. He later became a great Ächärya.
